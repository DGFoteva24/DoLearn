About the subject

Assigning real projects: Employers can provide tasks that reflect real-world problems, helping students practice coding in languages like C++, Java, Python, JavaScript, and more.




Providing feedback on code: They can review solutions, suggest improvements, and teach best practices for writing clean, efficient, and maintainable code.




Demonstrating problem-solving techniques: Employers can show how they approach debugging, algorithms, and optimization in professional projects.




Hosting coding workshops or tutorials: Students can watch professionals solve programming challenges step by step, learning how to apply different languages effectively.




Linking theory to practice: Employers can explain how concepts like loops, functions, data structures, and object-oriented programming are applied in real applications.




Tracking progress and mentoring: Through the platform, employers can monitor student work, provide personalized guidance, and suggest learning paths for multiple languages.
