About the subject

Employers can use an interactive platform to share their real-world experience with students and guide their learning in several ways:




Sharing practical tasks and projects: Employers can post real-world assignments that reflect industry standards, giving students hands-on experience.




Providing feedback: Through the platform, employers can review students’ work, suggest improvements, and highlight best practices.




Hosting Q&A sessions or webinars: Students can ask questions about industry challenges, workflows, or tools, and learn directly from professionals.




Offering mentorship through profiles: Employers can create profiles detailing their expertise and areas where they can guide students.




Tracking progress: The platform can allow employers to monitor student development, giving advice based on observed strengths and weaknesses.




Connecting theory to practice: Employers can link academic concepts to real projects, helping students understand how HTML, CSS, and other skills are used in the workplace.
